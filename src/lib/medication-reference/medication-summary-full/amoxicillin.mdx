### High-Level Summary
Amoxicillin is a widely used penicillin-type antibiotic effective against a broad range of bacterial infections. It is commonly prescribed for ear, nose, and throat infections, as well as skin and urinary tract infections.

### Indications
- **Otitis Media**: Inflammation of the middle ear.
- **Sinusitis**: Inflammation of the sinuses.
- **Pharyngitis/Tonsillitis**: Throat infections caused by Streptococcus.
- **Skin and Soft Tissue Infections**: Caused by susceptible bacteria.
- **Urinary Tract Infections**: Such as cystitis.

### Contraindications
- **Penicillin Allergy**: Known history of a severe hypersensitivity reaction (e.g., anaphylaxis) to penicillins or cephalosporins.
- **Infectious Mononucleosis**: Associated with a high incidence of a non-allergic skin rash.

### Adverse Effects
- **Common**: Diarrhea, nausea, rash.
- **Less Common**: Vomiting, headache.
- **Rare but Serious**:
  - **Anaphylaxis**: Severe, potentially life-threatening allergic reaction.
  - **Clostridioides difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD)**: A severe form of diarrhea.
  - **Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS)**: A rare, serious disorder of the skin and mucous membranes.

### Pregnancy & Lactation
- **Pregnancy**: Generally considered safe for use during pregnancy (Category B).
- **Lactation**: Excreted in breast milk in small amounts; use with caution and monitor the infant for potential side effects like rash or diarrhea.
